About Me

Interests and qualifications

I am mainly interested in both theory and observations of transients and stellar interactions. My current focus is on stellar collisions, but I worked on cataclysmic variables and supernovae as well. I also have a strong interest in computational science, especially in the context of hydrodynamical simulations.

Projects

My research mainly focuses on transients, i.e. astronomical objects that undergo sudden and drastic changes. Depending on their nature, such events are observable at different wavelengths, they can last from milliseconds to years and can even reccur semi-periodically. I study different types of transients, including strong stellar interactions and collisions, cataclysmic variables, and thermonuclear supernovae.
