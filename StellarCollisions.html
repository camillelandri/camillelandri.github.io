Transients from stellar collisions

When two stars cross paths and end up colliding, they eject material which will later interact with the radiation from the collision product. These interactions will lead to a brightness increase, i.e. a transient. My goal is to predict these transients, and in order to do so I first need to simulate the collisions and obtain the amount of mass ejected. I will then use this mass as input into a Radiative Hydrodynamic code to study how the radiation emitted by the collision product will interact with the ejected matter.

For now I am working on the first part of this project: I wish to model the collision with Phantom, a Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) code. SPH is a technique that discretises the hydrodynamic equations by approximating a fluid as a collection of particles (a more classical approach would be to simulate a fluid evolving in a grid). Each particle has its own properties (position, velocity, energy, etc) and one can interpolate the variables of interest (e.g. density) to visualise the behaviour of the fluid(s). While this process has many downsides, it is a good approximation in highly dynamical contexts, like stellar collisions, which are computationally expensive in 3D grid-based Hydrodynamic codes.
