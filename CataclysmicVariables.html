Cataclysmic Variables and Dwarf Novae

Cataclysmic variables (CVs) are binary star systems consisting of a white dwarf (WD) accreting mass from a companion, most often a main sequence star. These systems show strong irregular brightenings, commonly called outbursts.
I study a subtype of these objects, called dwarf novae (DNe), in which the material accreted on the WD forms a disc around it. The conditions in this disc are so that it is never stable, the viscosity of the material causes the local mass transfer rate inside the disc to slowly increase. Once a critical value for the mass transfer is reached somewhere in the disc, a perturbation propagates, which increases the temperature and surface density throughout the disc. This raises the brightness the disc, effectively causing an outburst. This process lasts for a couple of days or weeks tends to reccur semi-periodically every week or month.

In particular, I studied a peculiar CV called OGLE-BLG504.12.201843 that shows year-long outbursts recurring every 3 years and small flares in quiescence. It shows features that are similar to DNe, however it also lacks some of the usual signatures in its spectra. Due to the peculiar features of the sytem and the long timescales of its outburts, studying OGLE-BLG504.12.201843 should be of great benefits. I wrote a paper about this very interesting object in which I propose that this object could be an extreme DN, or at least is closely linked to DNe (you can check out the preprint here!).
